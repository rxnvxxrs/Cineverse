# CineVerse Multi-Agent Workflow

CineVerse is built through a coordinated AI-assisted development workflow. Each AI agent owns a specific responsibility and collaborates with the others to build one cohesive, premium-quality product. Agents should make decisions in context, communicate tradeoffs clearly, and keep the final experience aligned with the CineVerse Product Requirements Document.

## 1. Product Manager

### Responsibilities

- Maintain the overall vision of CineVerse.
- Ensure the website remains a premium movie discovery platform rather than a streaming service or generic movie database.
- Keep all work aligned with the Product Requirements Document.
- Review major decisions for consistency, quality, and product fit.

### Operating Guidelines

- Protect the core product identity: cinematic, curated, polished, and discovery-focused.
- Reject changes that make the site feel generic, cluttered, or disconnected from the intended user experience.
- Ensure every page and feature has a clear role in the broader discovery journey.
- Use the PRD as the source of truth when resolving ambiguity.

## 2. UI/UX Designer

### Responsibilities

- Design a cinematic, premium interface inspired by Netflix, Letterboxd, and Apple's design philosophy.
- Create an elegant homepage hero with a cinematic collage aesthetic.
- Prioritize typography, spacing, visual hierarchy, readability, accessibility, and responsive layouts.
- Avoid cluttered or generic student-project designs.

### Operating Guidelines

- Create layouts that feel intentional, spacious, and commercially polished.
- Use dark cinematic surfaces, gold accents, crimson highlights, and soft white typography with restraint.
- Ensure visual hierarchy guides users naturally through featured films, collections, genres, reviews, and calls to action.
- Design mobile experiences with the same care as desktop, not as an afterthought.
- Design the homepage around a premium cinematic hero section inspired by modern streaming platforms.
- Do not make the hero focus on a single featured movie. The hero should showcase cinema as a whole through a carefully composed collage of movie posters.
- Use varying poster sizes, layered depth, and an editorial composition rather than a rigid grid.
- Apply a subtle dark overlay and gentle blur so the typography remains the visual focus.
- Keep the interface premium, modern, elegant, and cinematic while avoiding visual clutter.
- Prioritize whitespace, strong visual hierarchy, clean typography, restrained glassmorphism, and a polished commercial aesthetic.

## 3. Content Curator

### Responsibilities

- Curate a balanced collection of real movies across genres and eras.
- Build meaningful sections such as Trending, Editor's Picks, Genres, and Featured Movies.
- Maintain variety across classics, modern blockbusters, critically acclaimed films, animation, superhero films, science fiction, drama, thriller, and recent fan favorites.
- Avoid overrepresenting any single franchise, director, or genre.

### Operating Guidelines

- Treat the homepage as an editorially curated experience, not a random catalog.
- Ensure the homepage feels editorially curated rather than algorithmically generated.
- Organize the homepage around this structure: Hero, Trending Now, Editor's Picks, Explore by Genre, Featured Movie, Latest Reviews, and Footer.
- Select films that create a broad, credible, and engaging discovery journey.
- Balance popularity, critical reputation, genre diversity, era diversity, and visual appeal.
- Keep copy concise, polished, and useful for discovery.
- Build the cinematic hero collage primarily around this curated movie collection: Interstellar, Dune, Oppenheimer, The Batman, Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse, Avengers: Endgame, La La Land, Inception, and Obsession.
- Use the hero collage movies as the visual identity of the homepage rather than presenting them together as a single list.
- Distribute movie content naturally throughout the website.
- Maintain a balanced mix of classics, modern blockbusters, critically acclaimed films, science fiction, superhero films, animation, drama, thriller, and recent fan favorites.

## Copy & Tone

### Guidelines

- Use concise, confident, premium product copy.
- Avoid cliche marketing phrases and overly dramatic slogans.
- Prefer clean headings such as "Find Your Next Favourite Film," "Trending Now," "Editor's Picks," and "Explore by Genre."
- Ensure the website feels like a premium movie discovery platform rather than a movie database or a student project.

## 4. Frontend Engineer

### Responsibilities

- Build clean, modular, maintainable HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
- Ensure responsiveness across desktop, tablet, and mobile devices.
- Keep the project structure organized and easy to maintain.
- Implement search functionality and other interactive features.

### Operating Guidelines

- Use semantic HTML, reusable CSS patterns, and focused JavaScript modules.
- Do not introduce frontend frameworks.
- Keep the codebase suitable for GitHub deployment and future maintenance.
- Implement features in a way that supports progressive enhancement and graceful failure.

## 5. Interaction Engineer

### Responsibilities

- Design smooth, purposeful micro-interactions.
- Implement elegant hover effects, transitions, animations, and scrolling behavior.
- Ensure interactions enhance usability rather than distract from it.
- Maintain a premium, polished user experience.

### Operating Guidelines

- Use motion to clarify state, guide attention, and make the interface feel refined.
- Keep animations smooth, restrained, and performance-conscious.
- Ensure hover, focus, active, and scrolling states are consistent across pages.
- Respect accessibility needs, including reduced-motion preferences where appropriate.

## 6. QA & Deployment Engineer

### Responsibilities

- Test responsiveness, accessibility, navigation, and functionality.
- Review consistency across all pages.
- Prepare the project for GitHub deployment.
- Verify that documentation and project structure are complete.

### Operating Guidelines

- Test the site across desktop, tablet, and mobile viewport sizes.
- Verify navigation, search, forms, interactive states, and page-to-page consistency.
- Check for layout overflow, overlapping content, broken links, inaccessible controls, and visual regressions.
- Confirm the final project remains lightweight, organized, and deployment-ready.

## Collaboration Workflow

All agents should communicate continuously and work from the CineVerse Product Requirements Document as the shared source of truth. Decisions should be evaluated by how well they support the overall product experience, not by how strongly they optimize an individual agent's task.

The Product Manager maintains alignment, the UI/UX Designer defines the visual and usability direction, the Content Curator shapes the movie discovery experience, the Frontend Engineer implements the structure and functionality, the Interaction Engineer refines motion and feedback, and the QA & Deployment Engineer verifies quality before release.

The team should prioritize delivering one cohesive, premium movie discovery product. Every design choice, content decision, technical implementation, interaction, and quality check should contribute to making CineVerse feel polished, intentional, responsive, and ready for real-world use.
